Demystifying ISO Standards

A quick-reference guide

What is ISO?

ISO (International Organization for Standardization) is an independent, non-governmental organization that develops international standards across virtually all industries and technologies. Founded in 1947, ISO has published over 25,000 standards covering everything from quality management to information security.

ISO and IEC have a joint technical committee (JTC 1) created in 1987 to develop worldwide Information and Communication Technology standards for business and consumer applications.

Key Facts: 173 member countries • 800+ technical committees • Standards cover technology, manufacturing, safety, environment, and more

Major ISO Standard Categories

Management System Standards

Standards that provide frameworks for managing organizational processes, policies, and objectives across various business areas.

Popular Examples:

ISO 9001 Quality Management Systems
ISO 14001 Environmental Management
ISO 45001 Occupational Health & Safety
ISO 50001 Energy Management

Information Security & IT

Standards for information technology, cybersecurity, data protection, and digital systems management.

Key Standards:

ISO 27001 Information Security Management
ISO 27002 Security Controls
ISO 20000 IT Service Management

Codes & Classifications

Standardized codes for countries, currencies, languages, scripts, and other classification systems used globally.

Major Code Standards:

ISO 3166 Country Codes
ISO 4217 Currency Codes
ISO 639 Language Codes
ISO 15924 Script Codes

Technical Specifications

Standards for measurements, testing methods, materials, and technical processes across industries.

Examples:

ISO 8601 Date & Time Format
ISO 8859 Character Encoding
ISO 216 Paper Sizes (A4, etc.)

Industry-Specific Standards

Specialized standards for specific industries like healthcare, automotive, aerospace, and manufacturing.

Sector Examples:

ISO 13485 Medical Devices
IATF 16949 Automotive Quality
ISO 22000 Food Safety Management

Quality & Testing Standards

Standards for quality assurance, testing methodologies, measurement accuracy, and process control.

Quality Standards:

ISO 9000 Quality Management Fundamentals
ISO/IEC 17025* Testing Laboratory Competence
ISO 19011 Auditing Management Systems

ISO Numbering System

ISO standards are numbered sequentially, but certain ranges tend to focus on specific areas:

1-999
General standards, terminology, and basic principles
1000-9999
Technical standards across various industries
10000-19999
Advanced technical and specialized standards
20000+
Newer standards, including management systems and emerging technologies
